Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 23, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how MACULINX ("we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, and discloses your information when you use our website (tickzero.maculinx.com). We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring compliance with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

1. Data Usage & Collection

TickZero is an open-source software application. The software itself runs locally on your machine and does not transmit personal gameplay data to our servers. However, when you visit our website, we use certain third-party services to improve user experience and analyze traffic.

2. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services which may collect and process data:

2.1 Google Analytics 4 (via Google Tag Manager)

We use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to understand how visitors interact with our website. GA4 collects data such as:

  • Pages visited and time spent on pages
  • Browser type and operating system
  • Approximate geographic location (country/city level only; IP addresses are anonymized)
  • Referral sources

Usage data is aggregated and anonymized. We do not use Google Analytics to track individual users across different websites.

2.2 Google Fonts

Our website uses Google Fonts to display text. When your browser requests these fonts, your IP address is transferred to Google's domains (fonts.googleapis.com and fonts.gstatic.com). This is necessary for the proper rendering of the website. Google may store this data for analytics and abuse prevention.

3. Cookies

We use cookies to enhance your experience. Cookies are small text files stored on your device.

  • Essential Cookies: Necessary for the website to function (e.g., remembering your cookie consent preferences).
  • Analytics Cookies: Used by Google Analytics to track website performance (only activated with your consent).

You can manage or delete cookies via your browser settings at any time.

4. Data Retention

Data collected via Google Analytics is retained for a period of 14 months, after which it is automatically deleted. We do not store any personal data on our own servers.

5. Your Rights (GDPR)

Under the GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to Access: You can request information about the data we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification: You can request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Right to Erasure: You can request deletion of your data ("Right to be forgotten").
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You can request that we limit how we use your data.
  • Right to Object: You can object to the processing of your data.

To exercise these rights, please contact us via the GitHub repository issues page.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date.

7. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please contact us via our GitHub Repository.
